Connection Types

Solar System Types

ON GRID
Connected System

On-Grid Solar

On-grid (or grid-tied) solar systems are connected directly to the public electricity grid. They are the most common and cost-effective type of solar installation for homes and businesses with reliable grid access.

Excess power generated is exported back to the grid, and you earn credits through net metering — effectively making your meter run backwards during sunny hours.

  • No battery required — lowest upfront cost
  • Net metering: earn credits for excess energy
  • Ideal for urban homes & commercial buildings
  • Seamless grid backup during low solar hours
  • High efficiency — no energy wasted
OFF GRID
Standalone System

Off-Grid Solar

Off-grid solar systems operate completely independently from the electricity grid. They rely on battery banks to store solar energy for use at night or during cloudy days — perfect for remote locations.

These systems provide complete energy autonomy, protection from tariff hikes, and reliable power in areas where grid connectivity is unavailable or unreliable.

  • Complete independence from the utility grid
  • Large battery storage for round-the-clock power
  • Ideal for remote farms, resorts & rural areas
  • Protection from grid outages & power cuts
  • Zero electricity bills — fully self-sufficient
HYBRID
Best of Both Worlds

Hybrid Solar

Hybrid solar systems combine the benefits of both on-grid and off-grid setups. They are connected to the grid while also having battery storage, giving you maximum flexibility and resilience.

Use solar first, store the excess in batteries, and draw from the grid only when necessary. During outages, your batteries seamlessly take over — keeping critical loads powered.

  • Battery storage + grid connection together
  • Uninterrupted power during grid outages
  • Maximize self-consumption & net metering
  • Intelligent energy management systems
  • Future-ready — add more batteries anytime
